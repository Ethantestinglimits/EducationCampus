Dwayne Johnson could work as much as 50 children if not more! This challenge boosted our motivation over 100% that what it was and we are again asking you to help this cause be known for Togo’s kids. Testimonies
Kofi Lawson, 22y : “When I was younger, my mom used to teach me how to talk, read, walk and all the basic knowledge a child needs. But as I grew up, I soon realised that this knowledge was pretty limited. I wanted to learn more, but I often didn’t have the time because my dad wanted me to help him in the fields. When I turned 18 year old, I came to Europe and at this moment, I understood how the lack of knowledge can put you on the fringe of society. I don’t regret helping my parents because they really needed it, but what I regret is that it forced me to stop learning.”
Ayawa Kamara, 7y : “Everyday, when I wake up, my mom and I are making breakfast for the men. They wake up soon after us, eat breakfast, dad kisses mom on the cheek and me on the forehead, and he and my brother go to work in the field for the day. All morning, me and my mom are cooking the meal, and traveling to different wells to bring back water. When the evening comes, after dinner, my dad sometimes reads books for the family before we sleep. I would love reading, but the days are too busy and I just don't have time. But when I'm older, I will know how to read, and I will teach others how to do so !”
